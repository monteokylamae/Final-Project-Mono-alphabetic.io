What is Monoalphabetic Substitution Cipher?

Monoalphabetic substitution cipher is a basic substitution cipher where each letter in the plaintext is substituted with a corresponding letter from the cipher alphabet. In this cipher, the relationship between the plaintext and the ciphertext is based on a fixed mapping of the letters of the alphabet.

Symmetric Algorithm?

A symmetric algorithm, also known as a symmetric-key algorithm or a secret-key algorithm, is a type of encryption algorithm that uses the same key for both encryption and decryption of data. In symmetric encryption, the sender and receiver share a common secret key that is used to transform the plaintext into ciphertext and vice versa.
